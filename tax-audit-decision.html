Section 44AB thresholds at a glance

  • Business — clause (a): Tax audit mandatory if turnover > ₹1 crore. Threshold raised to ₹10 crore if cash receipts and cash payments do not exceed 5% of total.
  • Profession — clause (b): Tax audit mandatory if gross receipts > ₹50 lakh.
  • Section 44AD — clause (e): Audit triggered for a business taxpayer who earlier opted into Section 44AD, then opts out, and total income exceeds basic exemption — lock-in for 5 years.
  • Section 44ADA — clause (d): Audit triggered for a profession declaring profit lower than 50% of receipts when total income exceeds the basic exemption.
  • Companies Act statutory audit: Every company (Pvt Ltd, Public Ltd, OPC, Section 8) regardless of turnover, under Section 139 of the Companies Act, 2013.
  • LLP audit: Mandatory if turnover > ₹40 lakh or contribution > ₹25 lakh (Section 34(4) of the LLP Act, 2008).

Filing deadline: Tax audit report (Form 3CA / 3CB and 3CD) is due by 30 September following the financial year (extended date applies for transfer pricing audit cases). Penalty under Section 271B for non-compliance is 0.5% of turnover, capped at ₹1.5 lakh.
